# CheckGlyphs Morphology Audit — Glyph Engine 2.3

## Scope

This audit evaluates whether Glyph Engine 2.3 behaves as a deterministic transduction rather than as an unconstrained symbol generator. It specifically tests the revision prompted by two failures observed in Engine 2.2:

1. identical or near-identical color bands collapsed into nearly identical silhouettes;
2. the vocabulary occupied a narrow, overly dense region of the 5×5 lattice.

The audit script is reproducible with:

```bash
node scripts/morphology-audit.mjs
```

Its raw output is stored in `morphology-audit-results.json`. The galleries are generated by `scripts/morphology-gallery.mjs`.

## Architectural change

Engine 2.3 replaces the single-phenotype-plus-one-cell-inflection model with two deterministic layers:

```text
chromatic behavior → admissible morphology field
on-chain identity  → one realization inside that field
```

The chromatic descriptor still controls the semantic region: primary and neighboring families, density envelope, symmetry tendencies, topology, orientation, and interior tendencies. Contract/token identity and local Check index may select different archetypes, transforms, compatible families, densities, and accents, but cannot leave that behavior-defined neighborhood.

This is intentionally not the rule `same behavior → same silhouette`. The active rule is:

```text
same behavior → same field, multiple related silhouettes
```

## Method

The audit generated:

- 1,000 deterministic glyphs from synthetic tracks containing one to nine colors;
- 200 pairs differing by one RGB unit;
- 200 pairs differing by `0.001` in one normalized timing value;
- 200 pairs of unrelated tracks;
- 300 identities sharing one exact chromatic track;
- five independent 80-Check groups;
- one 80-Check monochromatic group;
- one 80-Check group sharing the same multi-color closed band.

Measurements include exact silhouette uniqueness, Hamming distance on the 25-cell mask, Jaccard overlap, family preservation, visible-cell count, connected components, isolated accents, symmetry, diagonal dominance, traversal jumps, and collision retries.

## Density and vocabulary

Across 1,000 varied signals:

| Measure | Result |
|---|---:|
| Distinct silhouettes | 572 / 1,000 |
| Mean visible cells | 5.495 |
| Minimum / maximum | 2 / 12 |
| 10th percentile | 3 cells |
| 25th percentile | 4 cells |
| Median | 5 cells |
| 75th percentile | 7 cells |
| 90th percentile | 8 cells |
| Glyphs with ≤4 cells | 36.3% |
| Glyphs with ≤6 cells | 73.2% |

The output therefore no longer concentrates around one medium-dense silhouette class. Minimal signs, broken bars, paired cells, compact stems, and larger developed forms coexist inside the same engine.

The 572 exact silhouettes observed here are not a vocabulary ceiling. They are the unique masks reached by this finite sample after semantic constraints and collision policy are applied.

## Controlled topology

Engine 2.3 deliberately permits some discontinuity because detached dots, paired modules, and satellites are part of the intended glyph language.

| Components | Glyphs in the 1,000 sample |
|---|---:|
| 1 | 475 |
| 2 | 507 |
| 3 | 18 |

No glyph exceeded three components or two isolated accents. `38.0%` contained at least one isolated accent. This is a controlled vocabulary feature, not unrestricted noise: topology mode, component ceilings, balance checks, and density bounds constrain every detached element.

Exact vertical symmetry occurred in `19.8%` of the sample. The engine therefore avoids the vertical-symmetry dominance found in earlier versions. Diagonal dominance occurred in `21.7%`; this is higher than Engine 2.2 because sparse stair and rune forms are now valid, but it is distributed across a broader family vocabulary rather than imposed as a default axis.

Traversal jumps occurred in `76.9%` of sampled glyphs. This is expected for accented and split glyphs: the traversal remains deterministic and visits each visible cell once, but cannot be orthogonally continuous across deliberately separated components.

## Local semantic stability

| Perturbation | Mean Hamming distance | Identical shape | Same realized family |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| One RGB unit | 0.315 cells | 95.0% | 95.5% |
| `0.001` normalized timing | 0.390 cells | 93.0% | 93.5% |
| Unrelated tracks | 6.095 cells | 0.5% | 6.5% |

The medians for both small perturbation tests are zero. Threshold boundaries still exist—as they must in a finite discrete system—and a small minority of near-boundary inputs can move farther. The distributions nevertheless show a clear separation between local edits and unrelated behavior.

## Identity diversity inside one field

Across 300 contract/token/index identities sharing the same exact track:

- 200 distinct silhouettes were produced;
- five compatible realized families were used;
- the mean Hamming distance from the first realization was 4.57 cells;
- the maximum observed distance was 11 cells.

This is a deliberate reversal of Engine 2.2’s identity bound. Identity now resolves a member of a chromatic field rather than adding or removing one cell from a mandatory phenotype. The chromatic behavior key and primary field remain unchanged across all 300 identities.

## Uniform-band stress tests

### Monochromatic 80-Check group

All 80 Checks share the primary `bar` field, yet the engine produced 79 distinct silhouettes. The visible-cell distribution was:

- mean: 4.275;
- minimum: 2;
- median: 4;
- 90th percentile: 6;
- maximum: 7.

Compatible realized families included `bar`, `emblem`, `scatter`, `rune`, `monolith`, and `gate`.

### Identical multi-color closed band

All 80 Checks share the primary `knot` field. The engine produced 80 distinct silhouettes with only five collision retries in total.

| Measure | Result |
|---|---:|
| Distinct silhouettes | 80 / 80 |
| Realized compatible families | 5 |
| Minimum / maximum cells | 4 / 12 |
| Mean cells | 7.275 |
| Median cells | 7 |
| Minimal glyphs (≤4 cells) | 4 |
| Compact glyphs (≤6 cells) | 30 |
| Developed glyphs (≥8 cells) | 31 |
| One-component glyphs | 43 |
| Two-component glyphs | 37 |

This test directly addresses the Color Band 1 failure mode: identical chromatic behavior no longer forces repeated or near-repeated forms, and the band contains both sparse and developed signs.

## Collision behavior

Across five groups of 80 varied tracks, all groups reached 80 exact silhouettes. Of 400 glyphs, 372 required no retry and 28 required one retry. No group retained an exact duplicate.

The retry mechanism does not inject runtime randomness. It deterministically explores another realization within the same chromatic field and remains capped at 18 attempts.

## Interpretation of the central claim

The statement

> CheckGlyphs does not invent a symbol for a Check. It reveals a glyph latent within its chromatic behavior.

is defensible in Engine 2.3 when “latent glyph” is understood as a **behavior-defined field of admissible forms**, not as one unique silhouette mathematically recoverable from color alone.

The source behavior determines what kind of glyph may exist. On-chain identity resolves which member of that field becomes visible. The result is neither free decoration nor a one-to-one encoding of color into shape.

A maximally literal technical formulation is:

> CheckGlyphs reveals a morphological field latent within chromatic behavior and deterministically resolves one glyph within it through on-chain identity.

## Verdict

Engine 2.3 corrects the two principal weaknesses of Engine 2.2:

- it restores broad typological and density variation, including genuinely minimal glyphs;
- it prevents identical bands from collapsing into one repeated form.

It also retains the strongest correction introduced by Engine 2.2: small chromatic edits no longer behave statistically like unrelated tracks.

The system is publishable in this form. It is not mathematically perfect—finite thresholds, 25 binary positions, and bounded collision search make absolute continuity and universal uniqueness impossible—but its trade-offs are now aligned with the artistic and technical thesis of CheckGlyphs.
